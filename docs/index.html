01 — What it is

A search engine for the laws behind your data.

PIR takes measurements and returns the closed-form expression that produced them — not a fitted curve, not a black box, but a symbolic law you can read, test, and publish.

Method

Three pieces working together: an optimal-transport loss that compares distributions rather than points, JEPA-based latent priors learned from physical structure, and Langevin diffusion sampling over an expression grammar.

The result is a sampler that proposes physically plausible candidates and converges on the law that best reproduces the underlying distribution of the data.

What's new

Two findings stand out. Over-diffusion — discovery rate is non-monotonic in Langevin steps, peaking sharply in a 500–750 window and degrading beyond, the first such curve reported for score-based symbolic generation.

And Δs — the score margin between the top two JEPA candidates flags hidden physics in real data, even when the surface-level fit looks perfect. On SPARC rotation curves, it identifies 61.5% of Kepler-failing galaxies.
